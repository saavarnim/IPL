Understanding the CAPTCHA Challenges

We offer a variety of puzzle types that ensure both usability and security. Each puzzle is designed to be easy for humans to solve, yet difficult for bots.

1. Color Matching Puzzle

Description: In this puzzle, you will drag colored boxes and drop them into the correct positions based on the sequence.

How to Solve: Simply drag and drop the colors to match the predefined order.

Example Puzzle: Match the colors in the order of green, blue, and red.

2. Shape Matching Puzzle

Description: Here, you will match different shapes with corresponding drop zones.

How to Solve: Drag each shape to its matching outline (circle, square, triangle) and drop them in place.

Example Puzzle: Place the triangle in its outlined zone, the square in another, and so on.

3. Drag-and-Drop Puzzle

Description: This puzzle involves arranging a set of pieces into the correct order or location by dragging them.

How to Solve: Drag each piece into the correct position based on the puzzle instructions.

Example Puzzle: Arrange the letters in the order: B, A, C.

4. Number Puzzle

Description: Similar to the drag-and-drop puzzle, but this time the challenge is arranging numbers.

How to Solve: Drag the numbered pieces to the correct order.

Example Puzzle: Arrange the numbers: 3, 1, 2.



How Our CAPTCHA System Works

1.Human vs. Bot Differentiation: Our system doesn't rely solely on solving the puzzles. We monitor your behavior, such as:

  • Cursor Movement: Natural, human-like movement patterns are detected.
  • Time Taken: If the puzzle is solved too quickly or without interaction, it's flagged as a potential bot action.
  • Interaction Complexity: A mix of interactions like moving different elements or adjusting puzzle pieces is a clear sign of human behavior.

2.Multiple Puzzle Options: To keep things dynamic and secure, we store a variety of puzzles in the system. You can switch between puzzle types for each CAPTCHA challenge.

3.Error Handling: If a bot-like pattern is detected (rapid solving, little cursor movement, etc.), the user will be asked to try again, ensuring only human users pass through.
